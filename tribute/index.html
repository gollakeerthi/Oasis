Tribute to Mahatma Gandhi

About Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2, 1869, was an Indian lawyer, politician, and leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule. He is also known as Bapu (father) and the Father of the Nation in India. Gandhi employed nonviolent civil disobedience as a powerful tool for social and political change, inspiring movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

Gandhi's philosophy of truth, nonviolence, and peaceful resistance continue to influence people globally. He fought for India's independence through methods such as peaceful protests, fasting, and acts of self-sacrifice. His most notable campaigns included the Salt March, the Non-Cooperation Movement, and the Quit India Movement.

Mahatma Gandhi's legacy lives on, and his principles of truth, justice, and equality continue to inspire generations to strive for a better world.
